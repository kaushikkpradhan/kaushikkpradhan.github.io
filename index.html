About Me

Kaushik Pradhan is an applied seismologist specializing in distributed acoustic sensing (DAS), ambient noise interferometry, and near-surface and geothermal imaging. He earned his Ph.D. in Geological Sciences from the University of Texas at El Paso and has cultivated expertise in fiber-optic sensing, cross‑well DAS, and geophysical monitoring.

His research career has included positions at national laboratories such as Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (LBNL) and Pacific Northwest National Laboratory (PNNL), where he focused on energy infrastructure, carbon sequestration monitoring, and geophysical data analysis. Kaushik also has industry experience with MicroSeismic Inc. and has contributed to hydraulic fracture mapping and microseismic monitoring for unconventional reservoirs.

Kaushik’s work has been published in leading journals and presented at conferences such as AGU and SEG. He is passionate about translating seismological innovations into practical solutions for energy, environmental, and infrastructure challenges.
