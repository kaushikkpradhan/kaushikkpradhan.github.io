Kaushik Pradhan

I am a PhD candidate in Geological Sciences (Geophysics) at the University of Texas at El Paso and a final-year doctoral student, with expected graduation in Summer 2026. I am seeking a postdoctoral position starting August 2026.

My research focuses on near-surface and geothermal seismology, with an emphasis on imaging shallow crustal structure using ambient noise correlations, H/V spectral ratios, surface-wave dispersion, and scattering-based approaches.

My dissertation centers on the Valles Caldera in northern New Mexico, where I combine dense nodal seismic arrays to study shallow crustal structure and magmatic processes.

Through the NSFâ€“DOE Geothermal Internship Program, I have applied scattering-based methods to study geothermal reservoirs in national laboratory settings.

At Pacific Northwest National Laboratory, I worked on seismic data from the EGS Collab experiment at the Sanford Underground Research Facility, characterizing hydraulic fracture development using scattering cross-section density derived from coda-wave decorrelation of active-source data.

At Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, I analyzed seismic data from the Cape Modern geothermal site in Utah, using waveform envelope modeling of low-magnitude local events to separate intrinsic and scattering attenuation, and interpreting these patterns as indicators of small-scale heterogeneity and fracture development.

I also work with Distributed Acoustic Sensing (DAS) in collaboration with Dr. Marianne Karplus.

Advisor: Dr. Julien Chaput
