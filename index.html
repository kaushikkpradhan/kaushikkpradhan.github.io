Kaushik Pradhan

Applied Seismology · Distributed Acoustic Sensing · Geothermal Systems

I am a PhD researcher in seismology at the University of Texas at El Paso, working on passive and active seismic methods for imaging volcanic, geothermal, and engineered subsurface systems.

My research focuses on distributed acoustic sensing (DAS), ambient noise interferometry, coda-wave analysis, and Bayesian inversion of seismic observables. I develop physics-based workflows that integrate field data, numerical modeling, and uncertainty quantification.

I have conducted research at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory and Pacific Northwest National Laboratory through NSF-supported geothermal programs, and I have industry experience in microseismic monitoring and fracture characterization.

I am currently seeking postdoctoral positions in applied seismology, near-surface geophysics, and geothermal energy.
